## Post 2: Programmatic SEO is NOT Product-Led SEO

**Date:** February 2024

**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/schwartze_programmatic-seo-is-not-product-led-seo-activity-7160655947831373824-p8E9

### Summary

Eli argues that programmatic SEO is only useful when it supports a real product experience. Creating thousands of pages without considering the user's journey does not create sustainable organic growth.

### Key Takeaways

- User journey should guide SEO decisions.
- Programmatic pages alone do not create value.
- Product experience comes before scale.

### Frameworks

- Product-Led SEO

## Post 3: Rethinking SEO Roles for the AI Search Era

**Date:** 2026

**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/schwartze_who-should-you-hire-for-aeo-activity-7469074381382680576-M2a0

### Summary

Eli argues that future SEO professionals should think like product managers rather than traditional SEO specialists. AI Search requires strategic thinking, data interpretation and collaboration across product, engineering and content teams.

### Key Takeaways

- Modern SEO requires product thinking.
- AI Search rewards strategic execution.
- Cross-functional collaboration becomes essential.

### Frameworks

- Product Manager Mindset for SEO

# Overall LinkedIn Patterns

- SEO continues evolving instead of disappearing.
- Product experience drives sustainable organic growth.
- AI Search requires strategic rather than tactical SEO.
- User journey matters more than keyword volume.
- SEO teams should become product-oriented.

# Knowledge Extracted

## Mental Models

- SEO should solve user problems before targeting rankings.
- Product value creates long-term organic growth.
- AI changes interfaces, not customer intent.

## Best Practices

- Design SEO around the product experience.
- Optimize user journeys instead of individual pages.
- Treat AI Search as an extension of SEO.

## Actionable Ideas

- Map complete search journeys.
- Audit SEO from a product perspective.
- Align SEO with product and engineering teams.
