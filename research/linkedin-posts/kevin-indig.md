## Post 3: Two brands can run identical on-page optimization and get cited differently

**Date:** June 2026

**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/kevinindig_two-brands-can-run-identical-on-page-optimization-activity-7473336241837731841-Dl3f

### Summary

Kevin explains that AI systems rely heavily on external trust signals. Two websites with similar on-page SEO may receive completely different citation rates depending on their reputation across third-party sources.

### Key Takeaways

- AI citations depend on external authority, not only on-page optimization.
- Brand mentions across trusted publications increase citation probability.
- Entity reputation is becoming a core SEO asset.

### Frameworks

- Off-property Authority
- Entity Trust Signals

# Overall LinkedIn Patterns

- SEO is evolving from ranking optimization to visibility optimization.
- Topical authority is becoming a measurable competitive advantage.
- AI search rewards trusted entities more than perfectly optimized pages.
- Multi-platform presence is increasingly important for organic discovery.
- Data-driven frameworks replace intuition in modern SEO.

---

# Knowledge Extracted

## Mental Models

- Visibility matters more than rankings.
- Authority must be demonstrated across the web, not only on owned websites.
- SEO should be treated as an ecosystem instead of a single Google channel.

## Best Practices

- Track visibility across multiple search platforms.
- Measure topical authority against competitors.
- Build external trust through citations and authoritative mentions.

## Actionable Ideas

- Create a topical authority dashboard.
- Audit external sources frequently cited by AI systems.
- Expand SEO measurement beyond keyword rankings.
