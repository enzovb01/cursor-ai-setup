# Recurring Patterns

> Synthesized from Kevin Indig research (2025–2026). See `research/kevin-indig-synthesis.md`.

## Content Creation

- **Two-layer stack:** intent clusters define what to write; SERP metadata/schema defines how listings win the second impression
- **Hyper-specific briefs:** target precise buyer questions (long prompts), not just short-head keywords
- **Sales-call ingestion:** mine winning customer conversations before keyword tools for topic prioritization
- **Freshness SLA:** content <3 months old gets ~3× LLM citation lift — bake quarterly refresh into production
- **Interactive moats:** calculators, assessments, and tools AI can't synthesize beat text-only pages in zero-click environments
- **Verification gates:** automate generation; tier human review by claim risk (internal refresh → SME → legal)

## SEO Strategy

- **Visibility economy:** measure impressions, citations, mentions — clicks are a shrinking and biased signal
- **Engine-specific lanes:** 91% of AI citations are engine-exclusive; no single AEO strategy works everywhere
- **Three-metric AI visibility:** presence, portability, concentration — never blend into one score
- **Off-site as core SEO:** Reddit, YouTube, G2, publishers are validation layers AI systems reuse
- **AIO ≠ AI Mode:** browse/comparison optimization (SERP) vs shortlist acceptance (model layer) are separate jobs
- **Intent compression:** AIOs flatten SERP behavior across intent types — metadata matters more than intent-based SERP predictions
- **Trust before relevance:** brand authority is the first gate users apply, even on navigational queries
- **Prune before scale:** domain quality matters (SEOzempic) — delete/merge low-performing inventory before AI-accelerating output

## AI Usage

- **Bottleneck-only automation:** codify full workflow → automate the slowest step → keep strategy and verification human
- **Research-before-prescription:** original data (UX, clickstream, citations) should precede scaled AI content
- **Judgment is the bottleneck:** cost to build/content ↓; cost to verify flat — budget review time explicitly
- **Vibe-code MVPs:** marketers can prototype internal SEO tools and interactive assets; devs productionize
- **Don't replace people in messaging:** "AI replaces steps, not workflows" — credibility matters for B2B buyers

## Distribution

- **Cross-channel ops:** SEO must coordinate with PR, social, affiliate, and brand teams for off-site signals
- **Community-driven validation:** invest in Reddit threads, YouTube walkthroughs, forum participation as citation inputs
- **Digital PR for citations:** original research and data campaigns generate third-party mentions LLMs prefer for commercial prompts
- **Segment by age:** under-35 validates on Reddit/YouTube; over-50 prefers classic results — different platform emphasis
- **Product as marketing:** standalone free tools acquire better than ads when distribution channels compress
